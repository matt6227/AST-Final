Name Johannes Kepler
Country Germany
Born December 27, 1571
Died November 15, 1630
Contributions:
  • He is best known for his laws of planetary motion (The Law of Orbits,The Law of Areas, and The Law of Periods), which he developed through years of meticulous observation and mathematical analysis.

  • Kepler's laws provided a crucial step forward in our understanding of the solar system and the universe as a whole. They allowed astronomers to accurately predict the movements of the planets and other celestial bodies, and paved the way for future discoveries and advancements in the field of astronomy.

  • He was pioneer in the use of telescopes for astronomical observations and made key contributions to the development of optics.

  • Kepler was a skilled mathematician who made significant contributions to the fields of geometry and calculus. He developed a method for calculating the volume of solids with curved surfaces, and he also developed a method for finding the roots of equations.

Why They Were Forgotten:
  • later discoveries under Newton overshadowed Kepler’s work in the eyes of later astronomers.

  • Kepler’s work was difficult to understand as it was highly mathematical and theoretical, which could have led to being overlooked by astronomers who were more interested in observational work.

  • Kepler lived was during political and religious upheaval in Europe. Thus, it made it difficult for him to gain support for his work, and may have contributed to his work being overlooked by later generations.

  • Kepler’s work was written in Latin which was not widely spoken or understood by later astronomers.
